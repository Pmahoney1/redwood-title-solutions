Resolving complex title issues for properties across the Flathead Valley, from Kalispell to Whitefish and the shores of Flathead Lake.

Flathead County sits at the gateway to Glacier National Park, encompassing some of Montana's most desirable real estate. From the resort communities of Whitefish and Bigfork to the commercial hub of Kalispell and the expansive shoreline of Flathead Lake — the largest natural freshwater lake west of the Mississippi — this region draws seasonal residents, vacationers, and full-time transplants from across the country. That steady demand for property, combined with the area's unique history of homesteading, timber claims, and recreational development, creates a landscape where title complications are remarkably common.

Flathead County's real estate market is shaped by tourism and recreation. Seasonal properties change hands frequently, often through informal arrangements or family transfers that bypass proper documentation. Vacation homes inherited across generations may sit idle for years before anyone examines the chain of title. When these properties finally come to market, buyers and sellers alike discover clouds that have been accumulating for decades — missing probate proceedings, unreleased deeds of trust, boundary disputes tied to old survey methods, and tax liens from years of delinquent payments on forgotten parcels.

The rapid growth of communities like Whitefish and Bigfork has also created subdivision-related title issues. Properties that were once large rural tracts have been divided and re-divided, sometimes without proper platting or easement documentation. Water rights along Flathead Lake tributaries add another layer of complexity, as do conservation easements that may restrict development in ways not always reflected in the recorded title.

Redwood Title Solutions brings focused curative expertise to Flathead County property owners, lenders, and real estate professionals who need clear, insurable title. We understand the specific title challenges that arise in resort and recreational markets, and we have the tools and experience to resolve them efficiently.

Our Curative Title Services in Flathead County

Every title problem is different, but our approach is consistent: thorough research, precise diagnosis, and decisive action. We offer five core curative title services throughout Flathead County and the broader Flathead Valley.

Tax Lien Resolution

Delinquent property taxes are a frequent source of title clouds in Flathead County, particularly on seasonal properties and vacant land. Montana's tax lien process allows counties to sell tax lien certificates after taxes go unpaid, and if the property is not redeemed within the statutory period, the tax lien purchaser can apply for a tax deed. We work through the full redemption and resolution process — calculating outstanding amounts, coordinating with the Flathead County Treasurer, negotiating with lien holders, and ensuring that all tax obligations are satisfied so title can be cleared.

Probate Title Clearing

Many Flathead County properties have been in families for generations, passed down through informal agreements rather than proper estate proceedings. When a property owner dies without a will — or with a will that was never probated — the title remains legally clouded until the estate is properly administered. We facilitate the preparation of heirship affidavits, research intestate succession under Montana's Uniform Probate Code, locate missing heirs, and coordinate with probate courts to establish clear ownership chains.

Judgment Lien Resolution

Judgment liens can attach to real property and create obstacles to sale, refinance, or development. In Flathead County, we regularly encounter judgment liens from civil lawsuits, contractor disputes related to construction and renovation projects, and federal tax liens. We analyze lien priority, negotiate with creditors, and pursue releases or quiet title actions to remove these encumbrances from the title record.

Clouded Title Resolution

Clouded titles in Flathead County often stem from recording errors, missing signatures on deeds, unreleased mortgages from lenders that no longer exist, and boundary disputes arising from historical survey inconsistencies. We identify every cloud on title, determine the most efficient curative path, and coordinate the necessary corrective steps — whether that means obtaining corrective deeds, securing lien releases, or coordinating quiet title actions in the Eleventh Judicial District Court.

Title Search & Opinion

A comprehensive title search is the foundation of any successful transaction or curative effort. We conduct thorough chain-of-title research through Flathead County Clerk and Recorder records, court filings, tax records, and federal databases. Our detailed title analysis reports identify every defect and provide a clear roadmap for resolution, giving you the information you need to make confident decisions.

Montana Title Law: What Flathead County Property Owners Should Know

Montana's legal framework creates several unique considerations for property owners and anyone involved in a real estate transaction in Flathead County. Understanding these provisions is essential for navigating title issues effectively.

Montana Code Annotated & Recording Statutes

Montana is a race-notice recording state under Montana Code Annotated (MCA) Title 70, Chapter 21. This means that a subsequent purchaser who records first and had no notice of a prior unrecorded conveyance will take priority. In Flathead County, where properties sometimes change hands through informal arrangements or unrecorded contracts, this statute has significant implications for determining ownership priority. Ensuring that every instrument in the chain of title has been properly recorded with the Flathead County Clerk and Recorder is critical to establishing clear, defensible title.

Tax Lien Redemption: Montana's Three-Year Period

Under MCA 15-18-111, Montana provides a three-year redemption period after a tax lien certificate is issued. During this period, the property owner — or any party with an interest in the property — can redeem the tax lien by paying the delinquent taxes plus interest and penalties. This extended redemption period is longer than many states and creates a window during which title remains uncertain. For Flathead County properties with delinquent taxes, we track redemption deadlines, calculate accurate payoff amounts, and ensure that all tax obligations are resolved before the redemption period expires and a tax deed application can be filed.

Mineral Rights Complications

Montana has a long history of mineral extraction, and Flathead County is no exception. Mineral rights were frequently severed from surface rights during the homesteading and mining eras, creating split estates where one party owns the surface and another owns the subsurface minerals. These severed mineral interests can create significant title complications, particularly when the mineral rights holders are unknown, deceased, or have not recorded their interests. Under MCA 82-10-103, unused mineral interests may be subject to abandonment proceedings, but the process requires careful analysis. We research mineral right chains of title, identify outstanding mineral interests, and work to consolidate or clear these encumbrances.

Montana's Uniform Probate Code

Montana adopted the Uniform Probate Code (MCA Title 72), which governs how estates are administered and how property passes upon death. The UPC provides for both formal and informal probate proceedings, as well as small estate affidavits for estates below the statutory threshold. In Flathead County, where many properties have been held in families for generations, understanding the UPC is essential for clearing title on inherited properties. We navigate both testate and intestate succession, prepare the necessary documents for probate or non-probate transfers, and ensure that the chain of title accurately reflects the current ownership.
