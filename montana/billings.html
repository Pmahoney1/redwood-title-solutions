Billings is Montana's largest city and the economic engine of Yellowstone County. Positioned at the crossroads of the energy sector, agriculture, and regional commerce, Billings anchors a vast trade area that stretches across eastern Montana and into Wyoming and the Dakotas. The city's diverse economy — from oil refineries and pipeline infrastructure to cattle ranches and irrigated farmland — creates a property landscape where title issues are as varied as the land itself.

The energy industry is deeply woven into Yellowstone County's real estate. Oil and gas exploration in the Bakken formation and the Powder River Basin has driven decades of mineral rights transactions, lease agreements, and royalty assignments. Many properties in the Billings area carry severed mineral estates — situations where surface ownership was separated from subsurface mineral rights generations ago. These split estates create some of the most complex title chains in Montana, often involving dozens of fractional mineral interest holders scattered across multiple states, some of whom are deceased or cannot be located.

Agriculture also shapes the title landscape. Large ranch and farm properties in Yellowstone County have been subdivided over generations, with parcels transferred through handshake deals, family agreements, and informal divisions that were never properly documented. Water rights tied to irrigation districts add another layer of complexity, as these rights often run with the land but may not appear in the standard chain of title. When these agricultural properties transition to residential or commercial use, the accumulated title defects can be substantial.

Whether you are dealing with a mineral rights dispute on an energy-sector property, a probate cloud on an inherited ranch, or a judgment lien on a residential parcel, our team has the expertise to identify and resolve every defect standing between you and clear title.

Our Curative Title Services in Billings

We offer the full range of curative title services in Yellowstone County, tailored to the specific challenges of the Billings market.

Tax Lien Resolution

Tax delinquencies in Yellowstone County can stem from ownership disputes, financial difficulties in cyclical energy and agricultural markets, or simply from the challenges of managing large land holdings with multiple parcels. When taxes go unpaid, the county issues tax lien certificates that begin Montana's three-year redemption countdown. We work with property owners and interested parties to calculate exact redemption amounts, negotiate with tax lien certificate holders, and resolve all outstanding tax obligations so that title can be cleared before the tax deed process proceeds.

Probate Title Clearing

Inherited properties in the Billings area frequently carry probate-related title defects. Ranch and farm families often pass property through generations without formal estate administration, relying on family understanding rather than legal documentation. When a transaction finally requires clear title, the accumulated probate gaps can span multiple generations and involve dozens of potential heirs. We conduct thorough heir searches, prepare heirship affidavits, navigate Montana's Uniform Probate Code, and coordinate probate proceedings in the Thirteenth Judicial District Court to establish definitive ownership chains.

Judgment Lien Resolution

Billings' position as a regional commercial center means that judgment liens from business disputes, construction litigation, and financial obligations are a regular feature of the title landscape. Federal tax liens from the IRS and state tax warrants from the Montana Department of Revenue also arise frequently. We analyze lien priority positions, negotiate settlements with creditors, pursue lien releases, and when necessary coordinate quiet title actions to remove these encumbrances from the record.

Clouded Title Resolution

Clouded titles in Yellowstone County arise from a wide range of sources: deed errors in early land patents, unreleased oil and gas leases, boundary disputes between agricultural parcels, breaks in the chain of title from unrecorded transfers, and unreleased mortgages from defunct lenders. We methodically identify each cloud, determine the most efficient curative approach, and coordinate the necessary corrective steps — from simple affidavits to quiet title actions — to deliver a clean, insurable title.

Title Search & Opinion

Title searches in Yellowstone County require particular attention to mineral rights records, which are maintained separately from surface title records and can extend back to the original federal land patents. Our comprehensive search process covers the Clerk and Recorder's land records, mineral rights filings, district court records, federal lien databases, and water rights documentation. The resulting title analysis report provides a complete inventory of every defect, encumbrance, and outstanding interest, with specific recommendations for curative action.

Montana Title Law: What Billings Property Owners Should Know

Several Montana statutes and legal frameworks are particularly relevant for property owners and real estate professionals in the Billings area.

Montana Code Annotated & the Race-Notice Standard

Montana's race-notice recording statute under MCA Title 70, Chapter 21 determines priority among competing claims to real property. In Yellowstone County, where unrecorded mineral leases, informal land transfers, and legacy ownership arrangements are common, understanding the race-notice standard is critical. A subsequent purchaser who records without notice of a prior unrecorded interest prevails — making timely recording and thorough title searches essential for protecting ownership rights.

Tax Lien Redemption: The Three-Year Window

Under MCA 15-18-111, Montana provides property owners a three-year redemption period after a tax lien certificate is issued. During this period, the owner or any interested party can redeem the property by paying the delinquent taxes plus accrued interest and penalties. For Billings properties, where tax delinquencies can accumulate quickly on large commercial or agricultural parcels, understanding and managing this timeline is essential. We monitor redemption deadlines for our clients and ensure that all tax obligations are resolved within the statutory window.

Mineral Rights: Montana's Most Complex Title Issue

Mineral rights disputes are arguably the most common and most complex title issue in the Billings area. Montana law allows mineral rights to be severed from surface rights and conveyed independently, and this severance can persist indefinitely. Over generations, severed mineral interests become increasingly fractionated as they pass through estates and are divided among heirs. The result is properties where dozens — sometimes hundreds — of parties hold fractional mineral interests, many of whom are unknown or unreachable. Under MCA 82-10-103, mineral interests that have been unused for a specified period may be subject to abandonment proceedings, which can help consolidate fractional interests and clear title. We specialize in researching mineral right chains of title, locating interest holders, and coordinating with qualified professionals to pursue the remedies necessary to resolve mineral rights clouds.

Uniform Probate Code & Agricultural Estates

Montana's Uniform Probate Code (MCA Title 72) provides the framework for estate administration, including the transfer of real property upon death. In the Billings area, where agricultural and ranch properties are frequently held in families for generations, the UPC's provisions for intestate succession, heirship determination, and small estate administration are particularly important. We use every tool the UPC provides — from informal probate proceedings to formal adjudication of contested estates — to clear probate-related title clouds on Yellowstone County properties.
